Vehicle Buying Guide

Understanding Vehicle Financing

Learn how vehicle financing works and what lenders consider when reviewing a vehicle loan application.

← Back to Vehicle Buying Guides

For many people, financing is the most practical way to purchase a vehicle. Understanding the basics can help you make informed decisions and choose a payment plan that fits your budget.

How Vehicle Financing Works

When you finance a vehicle, a lender pays for the vehicle on your behalf, and you repay the loan over an agreed period through regular monthly payments.

What Affects Your Monthly Payment

Your payment is usually based on the vehicle price, down payment, interest rate, loan term, and the value of any trade in.

What Lenders Consider

Lenders may look at your credit history, income, employment, existing financial obligations, down payment, and the value of the vehicle being financed.

Why a Down Payment Helps

A down payment can reduce the amount you need to finance, lower your monthly payment, reduce total interest paid, and improve your chances of approval.

New Vehicle Financing

Financing a new vehicle is often easier than financing a used one because new vehicles come with warranty coverage and have more predictable value. This may lead to more flexible financing options.
