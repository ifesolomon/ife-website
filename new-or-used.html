Vehicle Buying Guide

New or Used? Which Is Right for You?

Compare new and used vehicles and decide which option best fits your budget, lifestyle and long term plans.

← Back to Vehicle Buying Guides

One of the biggest decisions when buying a vehicle is whether to choose a new or used one. The truth is, there is no single answer that fits everyone. The best choice depends on your budget, lifestyle, driving habits, and long term plans.

When a New Vehicle Makes Sense

A new vehicle may be the better choice if you plan to keep it for many years, want the latest safety and technology features, value full warranty coverage, prefer lower early maintenance costs, or want to be the first owner.

When a Used Vehicle Makes Sense

A used vehicle can be an excellent option if you have a smaller budget, want to reduce your purchase price, drive fewer kilometres each year, or are comfortable purchasing a vehicle with an established history.

Consider the Total Cost of Ownership

The purchase price is only one part of owning a vehicle. It is also important to consider insurance, fuel, maintenance, repairs, warranty coverage, financing costs, and resale value.

Think About Your Lifestyle

Ask yourself how many passengers you usually carry, how much cargo space you need, how far you drive each year, and whether you will use the vehicle mostly in the city or on the highway.

There Is No Wrong Choice

Both new and used vehicles can offer excellent value when they match your needs. Rather than asking which is better, it is more helpful to ask which is better for you.
